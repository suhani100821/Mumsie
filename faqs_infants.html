Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I feed my newborn?

+

Newborns typically need to be fed every 2-3 hours, or 8-12 times a day, both day and night.

When will my baby start sleeping through the night?

+

Most babies sleep 5-6 hour stretches by 3-6 months, but every baby is different. Establishing a bedtime routine can help.

How do I know if my baby is hungry?

+

Babies often show hunger cues by sucking on their hands, rooting, or becoming fussy. Feeding on demand is key.

Is it normal for my baby to cry a lot?

+

Yes, it’s normal for babies to cry, especially in the early months. However, if the crying seems excessive or there are other signs of discomfort, consult a doctor.

When can I start tummy time with my baby?

+

Start tummy time as early as 2 weeks old, gradually increasing the time as your baby grows to help strengthen neck and shoulder muscles.

How do I soothe my baby when they’re fussy?

+

Try swaddling, rocking, gentle shushing, using a pacifier, or giving them a warm bath. Sometimes just holding your baby helps calm them.

What’s the best way to burp my baby?

+

Hold your baby upright on your chest or on your lap and gently pat or rub their back until they burp. This can help relieve gas after feeding.

How do I know if my baby is getting enough milk?

+

Look for steady weight gain, plenty of wet and dirty diapers (at least 6 wet diapers a day), and contentment after feeds.

When can I start introducing solids to my baby?

+

Most babies are ready for solids around 6 months of age when they can sit up with support and show interest in food.

How do I keep my baby safe while sleeping?

+

Always place your baby on their back to sleep in a crib or bassinet with a firm mattress. Avoid pillows, blankets, and stuffed animals in the crib.
